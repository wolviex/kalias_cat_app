### Minigame Storyboard 4: "Loaf Cat’s Snack Stack"
**Focus:** Math / Resource Management / Fractions
**Characters:** Kalia and Loaf Cat
**Target Difficulty:** Navigator (Ages 5-6)

Loaf Cat is famously sedentary and loves to eat. In this game, the player helps Kalia fulfill specific "snack orders" to keep Loaf Cat’s hunger meter in the green.

---

| Panel | Scene / Visual Description | Action / Interaction (Player & UI) | Dialogue / Audio |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1: Setup** | **Scene:** Loaf Cat is sitting on a kitchen counter. A "hunger meter" (shaped like a baguette) is half-empty. A "order bubble" shows a picture of 3 fish crackers and 2 blueberries.<br><br>**Visuals:** Soft, appetizing colors. The UI shows a tray of various snacks at the bottom. | **Action:** The player taps the snack tray to begin. | **SFX:** A low, rumbling "tummy growl."<br><br>**Kalia:** "Loaf Cat is hungry! The player must drag items from the tray onto the plate.<br><br>**Visuals:** As items land on the plate, a counter appears above them (e.g., "1, 2..."). | **Action:** The player drags 3 fish and 2 blueberries. ---

### Producer’s Note on DDA (Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment)

* **Explorer (3-4):** Focuses on simple 1-to-1 matching. "Give Loaf Cat 3 treats." The player just taps any 3 treats.
* **Navigator (5-6):** Simple addition and "half vs. whole" concepts. "Give Loaf Cat 2 red treats and 2 green treats. How many total?"
* **Master (7-8):** Introduction to basic multiplication or more complex fractions. "Loaf Cat wants 1/4 of this pizza" or "Give Loaf Cat 3 sets of 2 crackers."
