We need to ground the educational concepts in clear, actionable storyboard panels. Given the constraints of a young user’s attention span and the specific requirements for self-regulation, we should prioritize the mechanics of the *interaction* over complex visual sequences.

We will focus the first sequence on the core emotional intelligence feature: **Self-Regulation and Calming.**

---

### Minigame Storyboard 1: "Calming the Zoomies"
**Focus:** Emotional Intelligence (EQ) / Self-Regulation
**Characters:** Kalia (Player Control) and Noodles the Cat
**Target Difficulty:** (Adjusts based on age tier; storyboard shows a balanced mid-tier interaction).

| Panel | Scene / Visual Description | Action / Interaction (Player & UI) | Dialogue / Audio |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1: Setup** | **Scene:** Interior of Kalia’s cozy living room. A "status bubble" icon above Noodles the Cat shows a jagged, orange lightning bolt. Noodles is bouncing wildly, leaving motion trails. Kalia is standing still, looking slightly concerned.<br><br>**Visuals:** Soft, warm colors. Simple UI overlays (Inventory button, Map button). | **Action:** The player moves Kalia closer to Noodles. | **SFX:** Fast-paced, high-pitched "pinging" sound associated with Noodles' energy. The BGM increases slightly in tempo. |
| **2: Trigger** | **Scene:** Kalia is now next to Noodles. A large, pulsing prompt (like a hand icon) appears over Noodles.<br><br>**Visuals:** A dialogue bubble from Kalia with a question mark. | **Action:** The user (Player) taps Noodles. | **Kalia:** "Oh no! Noodles has 'The Zoomies'! He’s *too* hyper. Can we help him feel calm?" |
| **3: Decision** | **Scene:** A modal pops up (a choice window). Two options are presented visually:<br><br>1.  A laser pointer toy (high energy icon).<br>2.  A soft, pulsing, light-blue cushion (calm energy icon). | **Action:** The player must drag and drop the *correct* response (the cushion) onto Noodles. The incorrect choice (laser) is greyed out. | **Kalia:** "Noodles is already very energetic. Should we give him another *fast* toy, or a *calm* spot?"<br><br>**SFX:** Gentle selection 'ding.' |
| **4: Transition** | **Scene:** The main game screen fades. We enter a dedicated 'Calm Space' minigame overlay. The environment shifts to a softer, blurry background (like looking through a soft lens). Kalia and Noodles are both visible. A large, translucent 'Breathe' lung icon appears centrally. | **Action:** Automatic transition. | **SFX:** The high-energy BGM fades out. A slow, deep, resonant 'ocean drum' sound begins. |
| **5: Haptic Breathing** | **Scene:** **(The core interaction).** A large blue circle (The "Breath Circle") is on the screen. Inside it, a smaller white ring pulses slowly. A prompt reads: "BREATHE IN..." as the ring expands. | **Action:** The player must place their finger on the screen and *hold* it during the inhalation (3 seconds), matching the expansion of the ring. Haptic feedback (vibration) pulses gently and increases as they hold. | **SFX:** Slow, deep, calming inhale sound.<br><br>**Kalia (Calm, slow voice):** "Breathe in... like a balloon." |
| **6: Release** | **Scene:** The ring stops expanding and begins to shrink. The prompt reads: "BREATHE OUT..." The Haptic feedback changes to a slow, fading vibration. | **Action:** The player must release their finger from the screen as the ring shrinks (4 seconds). | **SFX:** A soft, prolonged exhale sound. The 'ocean drum' sound fades.<br><br>**Kalia:** "...And breathe out... like a fluffy cloud." |
| **7: Success & Reward** | **Scene:** Back in the living room. The 'Zoomie' bubble over Noodles is gone. He is sitting calmly, purring (Zzz's floating). A standard "Happy" emoji appears briefly. A small XP chest appears, floating toward Kalia’s 'Purr-gress' bar. | **Action:** The player taps the XP chest. Automatic return to normal gameplay. | **SFX:** A soft, satisfied purring sound. A positive, light-hearted chime for the XP collect.<br><br>**Kalia:** "There we go! Great job, Noodles! We feel much calmer now." |

### Producer’s Note on DDA (Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment)

* **Explorer (3-4):** The 'Breath Circle' prompts are visually larger. The *duration* of the breath is shorter (2 seconds in, 3 seconds out). Tapping the wrong icon in Panel 3 provides a gentle 'try again' (the icon wiggles), but does not cause failure.
* **Navigator (5-6):** The sequence shown above. The duration is standard (3/4 seconds).
* **Master (7-8):** The sequence might require the player to *synchronize* their breathing over 3 successful cycles, each one slightly longer than the last, or to complete the cycle without releasing the finger prematurely.
